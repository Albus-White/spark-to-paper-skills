# Human Scientific Style

Apply this during the style pass (SKILL Step 7) **directly to the manuscript files in `./paper/`**.
The goal is prose that reads like a clear human researcher wrote it — precise, plain, and honest.

## Principles

- **Write like a clear human researcher.** Plain, direct, specific.
- **One sentence, one idea.** Split sentences that carry two or more claims.
- **Prefer short sentences.** Break long, clause-heavy sentences into shorter ones.
- **Remove redundant AI-style filler.** Cut phrases like "it is important to note that", "in
  today's world", "plays a crucial role", "a wide range of", "delve into".
- **Remove empty adjectives.** Cut "novel", "powerful", "comprehensive", "cutting-edge",
  "state-of-the-art" unless they are precise and supported.
- **Avoid repeated transition phrases.** Do not start many paragraphs with "Moreover",
  "Furthermore", "Additionally", "In addition". Vary or drop them.
- **Avoid grandiose claims.** No "revolutionary", "groundbreaking", "solves the problem of".
  Scope every claim to what was actually tested.
- **Avoid template-like paragraphs.** Do not reuse the same paragraph skeleton across sections.
- **Use precise subject–verb–object structure.** Name the actor and the action; prefer active
  voice where it is clearer.
- **Every paragraph starts with a clear point.** The first sentence states the paragraph's claim;
  the rest supports it.
- **Claim strength must match evidence.** Soften any claim the results only partially support
  (see `result_writing_patterns.md` and `claim_evidence_rules.md`).

## Important

- **Do not try to bypass AI detection.** The objective is clarity, accuracy, and scientific
  style — not evading detectors. Never distort meaning, hedge facts, or add noise to "sound
  human". Honest, precise writing is the only target.

## Quick edit checklist

- [ ] Each sentence carries one idea.
- [ ] No filler phrases or empty adjectives remain.
- [ ] Transitions are varied and not formulaic.
- [ ] No grandiose or unsupported claims.
- [ ] Each paragraph opens with its point.
- [ ] Claim strength matches the evidence in the results.
- [ ] Meaning is unchanged and accurate after editing.
